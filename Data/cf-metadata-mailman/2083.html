c. Aliases arising from the 'Modification of some existing CF standard
names for chemical constituents' thread raised by Christiane Textor.

All older 'aerosol' names will be aliased to 'ambient_aerosol' names:

aerosol_angstrom_exponent becomes
angstrom_exponent_of_ambient_aerosol_in_air

At the same time the definition of this name will be expanded to read as
follows:
'"Aerosol" means the suspended liquid or solid particles in air (except
cloud droplets). "Ambient aerosol" is aerosol that has taken up ambient
water through hygroscopic growth. The extent of hygroscopic growth
depends on the relative humidity and the composition of the aerosol. The
"Angstrom exponent" appears in the formula relating aerosol optical
thickness to the wavelength of incident radiation: T(lambda) =
T(lambda0) * [lambda/lambda0]**(-1 * alpha) where alpha is the Angstrom
exponent, lambda is the wavelength of incident radiation, lambda0 is a
reference wavelength, T(lambda) and T(lambda0) are the aerosol optical
thickness at wavelengths lambda and lambda0, respectively.'

atmosphere_content_of_sulfate_aerosol becomes
atmosphere_mass_content_of_sulfate_ambient_aerosol

At the same time the definition of this name will be expanded to read as
follows:
'"Content" indicates a quantity per unit area. "Aerosol" means the
suspended liquid or solid particles in air (except cloud droplets).
"Ambient aerosol" is aerosol that has taken up ambient water through
hygroscopic growth. The extent of hygroscopic growth depends on the
relative humidity and the composition of the aerosol.'

mass_concentration_of_sulfate_aerosol_in_air becomes
mass_concentration_of_sulfate_ambient_aerosol_in_air

At the same time the definition of this name will be expanded to read as
follows:

'Mass concentration means mass per unit volume and is used in the
construction mass_concentration_of_X_in_Y, where X is a material
constituent of Y. "Aerosol" means the suspended liquid or solid
particles in air (except cloud droplets). "Ambient aerosol" is aerosol
that has taken up ambient water through hygroscopic growth. The extent
of hygroscopic growth depends on the relative humidity and the
composition of the aerosol.'

4. Amendments to definitions of existing names

a) dry_deposition names

As a result of the addition of new turbulent_deposition and
gravitational_settling names for the ECHAM-Hammoz model, it has been
agreed that the definition of all dry deposition_names should contain
the following explanatory sentence:
'Dry deposition is the sum of turbulent deposition and gravitational
settling.' Corresponding sentences will be included in the
turbulent_deposition and gravitational_settling name definitions to make
clear the relationships between all these names.

b) sea_water_pressure

No definition currently exists for this name. Following discussion of
the CMIP5 ocean names a definition will be created for this name as
follows:
"'sea_water_pressure' is the pressure that exists in the medium of sea
water. It includes the pressure due to overlying sea water, sea_ice, air
and any other medium that may be present."

5. Modifications to standard names Guidelines document
N.B. Some of these changes may take a little longer to implement than
the update to the standard name table itself.

a. Arising from the GRIB 2 names thread

The existing 'Chemical Species' section is very out of date. I will add
all the currently agreed species names. The use of the 'expressed_as'
phrase in species names will be explained. In addition, this section
will state the decision we have reached regarding IUPAC names:
> Specifically, it has
> been agreed that IUPAC names should be used where practicable, but it
> is recognised that non-systematic IUPAC approved names, or on occasion
> other common names, will be preferable where these are widely used,
> shorter and unambiguous. Where non-IUPAC chemical names are used in
> standard names the definition of the name should include the IUPAC
> equivalent.

We currently have a section in the Guidelines entitled 'Transformations'
which gives a number of 'template' phrases that can be used within
standard names to describe mathematical quantities, for example,
change_over_time_in_X, derivative_of_X_wrt_Y. Jonathan suggested some
time ago (not on the list) that we could similarly include templates for
the processes associated with the chemical names. I will add a section
entitled 'Chemical phrases' and list in there all the currently agreed
phrases (such as
tendency_of_atmosphere_mass_content_of_X_due_to_emission).

The 'Chemical Species' and 'Chemical Phrases' sections will be turned
into separate documents that will be linked to from the Guidelines, but
which can then also be viewed separately.

b. Arising from the "Medium 'atmosphere'" thread

(i) Position of large-scale and local medium within the standard name.

Following on from the discussion of large-scale and local media (e.g.
'atmosphere' versus 'in_air') and their respective positions within the
standard name, it has been agreed that large-scale medium should always
appear as a prefix and local medium as a postfix. This is consistent
with existing atmosphere/ocean and in_air/in_sea_water standard names
(including the moles_of_X aliases that will be created in this update).

(ii) Agreement has been reached that new 'local media' of
in_cloud_water, in_cloud_liquid_water and in_cloud_ice will be useful
for describing dissolved chemical species.

(iii) Position of 'Transformation' in the standard name.

Although mathematical transformations such as change_over_time_in_X are
an established part of standard names, their position within the
standard name is not currently documented.

The standard name guidelines will therefore be amended in accordance
with points i-iii:

a) In the 'Qualifications' section, change the pattern for constructing
standard names from

[surface] [component] standard_name [at surface] [in medium] [due to
process] [assuming condition]

to

[transformation(s)] [surface] [component] [large-scale medium]
standard_name [at surface] [in local medium] [due to process] [assuming
condition]

b) Add a new section entitled 'Large-scale medium' following the
existing 'Component' section:

Large-scale medium

The medium throughout which a quantity applies: atmosphere, troposphere,
stratosphere, mesosphere, thermosphere, middle_atmosphere, ocean.

c) Alter the title of the existing 'Medium' section to 'Local Medium'
and alter the wording as follows:

Local medium

The local medium or layer within which an intensive quantity applies:
in_air, in_atmosphere_boundary_layer, in_sea_ice, in_sea_water, in_soil,
in_soil_water, in_cloud_water, in_cloud_liquid_water, in_cloud_ice.

d) The sentence "Successive transformations may be applied" at the
beginning of the Transformations section will be extended to read
"Successive transformations may be applied, e.g.
square_of_lagrangian_tendency_of_X, and the transformations are applied
to the data in right to left order."
