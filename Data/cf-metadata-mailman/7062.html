Nan,

I think most archived values of "sea_surface_salinity" are practical
salinity, not absolute salinity. How about if we leave
"sea_surface_salinity" as "0.001" (or actually IMHO should be "g/kg")
and deprecate the use of "sea_surface_salinity" in favor of the newer
concepts of "absolute salinity", "preformed_salinity" or "practical
salinity".

>From www.teos-10.org:

"Importantly, while Absolute Salinity (g/kg) is the salinity variable
that is needed in order to calculate density and other seawater
properties, the salinity which should be archived in national data
bases continues to be the measured salinity variable, Practical
Salinity (PSS-78). To avoid confusion while the use of Practical
Salinity in scientific publications is phased out, published values of
salinity should be specifically identified as being either Practical
Salinity with the symbol SP or Absolute Salinity with the symbol SA."

-Rich
