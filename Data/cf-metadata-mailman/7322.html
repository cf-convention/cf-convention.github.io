> The items below have come up in follow up discussions after the Nottingham meetings. Many of them warrant further discussion at the NetCDF session at the next OGC TC meeting in Sydney.

>
> Issue From the Nottingham Meeting Summary
>
>
>
> "OGC may investigate a standardised approach to encoding spatial coordinates and spatial referencing in netCDF." In practice, how do we to follow up on this issue? Is it an item for the netCDF SWG or an ongoing action in some other WG? Is it related to the more general OGC effort to ?standardize? the CRS specification, introducing N-Dimensions?
>
>
>
> ESA Prod Trees Project Completion
>
>
>
> ESA Prod Trees project is now complete and plans to contribute its work to the OGC NetCDF SWG. CNR has already given of presentations on that work in earlier OGC TC meetings. It will be useful to continue efforts to harmonize this and other ongoing efforts within the OGC SWG activity and establish extended conventions supporting the Geosciences (Earth Sciences) Community. Likewise it is important to continuing to ensure that these conventions do not conflict with other netCDF conventions such as CF. (Note that CF conventions are already established as OGC standards.)
>
>
>
> Related Projects
>
>
>
> Other projects include conventions and other netCDF-related development efforts, e.g.:
>
> ? ODIP (Ocean Data Interoperability Platform), an EU Horizon 2020 project.
>
> ? EarthCube IA: Advancing netCDF-CF for the Geoscience Community, a recently funded US NSF Earthcube project. . http://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=1541031 <http://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=1541031>
>
> ? the work of the the National Computational Infrastructure at the Australian National University.
>
> Key questions ares how to coordinate all these efforts and, especially, where do we find the resources to do so?
>
>
>
> Name of Recently Adopted OGC netCDF Extension Standard
>
>
>
> A suggestion has been made that the name of our recently adopted NetCDF spec which references the coverages spec as "GML coverage model" which, as per a decision at the Barcelona TC, has been renamed to "Coverage Implementation Schema" (CIS). The idea is to avoid confusion for those who might read the two standards. However, there is some question as to whether this is completely accurate since the netCDF extension standard involves more than the Coverage Implementation Schema.
