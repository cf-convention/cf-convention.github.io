Here are proposed definitions provided by my colleague Ad Stoffelen at KNMI:

-------------------------------

divergence_of_surface_downward_stress

The divergence is a signed scalar that represents the magnitude of the 2D
surface wind downward stress vector field's source (positive) or sink
(negative) at a given point. The surface called "surface" means the lower
boundary of the atmosphere. "Downward" indicates a downward flux of
momentum, which accelerates the lower medium along the vector and the upper
medium against the vector. The surface downward stress is the wind stress
on the surface.

--------------------------------

curl_of_surface_downward_stress

The curl is a signed scalar that represents the magnitude of the 2D surface
wind downward stress vector field's local anti-clock-wise rotation at a
given point; clockwise rotation is thus given as negative curl. The surface
called "surface" means the lower boundary of the atmosphere. "Downward"
indicates a downward flux of momentum, which accelerates the lower medium
along the vector and the upper medium against the vector. The surface
downward stress is the wind stress on the surface.
