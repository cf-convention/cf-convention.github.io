The definitions below for the term 'geostrophic' may need to be beefed up,
since they're self-referencing..

I had to resort to google: 'A geostrophic current is an oceanic flow in
which
the pressure gradient force is balanced by the Coriolis effect. The
direction
of geostrophic flow is parallel to the isobars, with the high pressure
to the
right of the flow in the Northern Hemisphere, and the high pressure to the
left in the Southern Hemisphere.'

That may be more than we need, but I do think we should expand the
definitions. Users sometimes have to look through these without having
time to check all the terms outside the CF standard names page.

Cheers - Nan

> unit m s-1
> "A velocity is a vector quantity. "Eastward" indicates a vector

> unit m s-1
