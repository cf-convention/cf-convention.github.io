b. Three aliases were added to clarify recently created nitrogen deposition names (originally proposed by Daniel Neumann).
These names were changed to clarify that they refer to the mass of only the nitrogen contained in the compounds.

c. Eight aliases were created to change 'sea_level' to 'mean_sea_level'.
This change was made to help clarify the difference between (instantaneous) sea surface height and mean sea level. In addition to creating the aliases the definitions were updated by removing the sentence 'sea_level means mean sea level, which is close to the geoid in sea areas' and replacing it with ' "Mean sea level" means the time mean of sea surface elevation at a given location over an arbitrary period sufficient to eliminate the tidal signals.' Thanks to Jonathan Gregory and Roy Lowry for working on these changes.

3. Modifications to definitions of existing names
a. Updates to _pm*_ definitions proposed by Daniel Neumann.
Daniel pointed out that all definitions of _pm*_ names described the aerosol particles as "atmospheric pollutants" but this is not correct in all cases, for example, sea salt is a naturally occurring aerosol. The definitions were updated to remove the reference to pollutants, e.g., the sentence ' "Pm1 aerosol" is an air pollutant with an aerodynamic diameter of less than or equal to 1 micrometer' was replaced with ' "Pm1 aerosol" means atmospheric particulate compounds with an aerodynamic diameter of less than or equal to 1 micrometer.'
