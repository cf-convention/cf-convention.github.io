Dear Jonathan,

-> 'atmosphere' synonymous to _in_air ?
The Guidelines for Construction of CF Standard Names
http://cf-pcmdi.llnl.gov/documents/cf-standard-names/guidelines
state for 'atmosphere' under 'Special Phrases':
'atmosphere: used instead of in_air for quantities which are large-scale
rather than local '
I agree with what you say, that 'atmosphere' is not synonymous to
_in_air, but not only concerning the spatial scales. For chemical names
there is another difference, and I was referring to this: atmosphere
means not-ocean, not-land, not-vergation, etc., in the Earth system, but
in the atmosphere. This includes in_air, in_clouds, in_precipitation,
etc. Hence, in_air is a sub-element of (_in)_atmosphere, at least this
is what I thought we had defined. Maybe I am wrong?

-> _atmosphere or _in_atmosphere?
I am sorry that I have not raised this issue earlier. Why are
moles_of_G treated differently? The main distinction between gases and
aerosols is that gases have a defined molar mass, whereas most aerosols
do not have.

Best regards,
Christiane
